Question

    Which of these 2D shapes cannot have an odd number of lines of symmetry?

  • k A rhombus
  • 1An equilateral triangle
  • 2A trapezium
  • 3A right-angled triangle
  • 4A hexagon

    • Answer

    A line of symmetry through a 2D shape divides it into two equal halves so that the shape on one side of the line is a mirror image of the shape on the other side of the line.

    An equilateral triangle has 3 lines of symmetry, so it has an odd number of lines of symmetry.

    Lastly, we recall that a rhombus has 2 pairs of parallel sides and all of its sides are equal in length. In general, a rhombus has exactly 2 lines of symmetry.

    The only exception to this is the special case where the rhombus is a square, when it has 4 lines of symmetry. Even so, both 2 and 4 are even numbers.

    Therefore, the only one of the given shapes that cannot have an odd number of lines of symmetry is a rhombus.
