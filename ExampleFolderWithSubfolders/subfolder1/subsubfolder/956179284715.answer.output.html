  • 1smooth seeds only
  • 2wrinkled seeds only
  • 3smooth and wrinkled seeds in a ratio of approximately

    • Answer

    The shape of the seeds of a pea plant is encoded by one pair of hereditary factors. The term “hybrid” indicates that the plant has one hereditary factor that codes for the recessive version of the characteristic and one hereditary factor that codes for the dominant version of the characteristic, indicated by “Ss.” S represents the factor that causes the dominant trait, and s represents the factor for the recessive trait. As the seed was hybrid for the trait of being smooth, S codes for a smooth appearance and s codes for a wrinkled appearance.
